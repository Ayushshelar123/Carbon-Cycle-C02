Carbon Cycle

Introduction

The carbon cycle is Earth's natural way of recycling carbon atoms through the atmosphere, biosphere, oceans, and geosphere.

Photosynthesis

Plants absorb carbon dioxide (CO2) and convert it into oxygen and glucose through photosynthesis. Animals and humans then consume these plants and release CO2 back through respiration.

Fossil Fuels & Human Impact

Burning fossil fuels like coal, oil, and gas releases stored carbon into the atmosphere, increasing CO2 levels and accelerating climate change.

Ocean Absorption

Oceans absorb a large portion of CO2 from the atmosphere, helping regulate Earth's temperature — but excessive absorption causes ocean acidification.

Decomposition

When organisms die, decomposers break them down and release carbon back into the soil and atmosphere, completing the cycle.
